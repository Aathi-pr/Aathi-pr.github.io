# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Creative studios, founders, recruiters, and collaborators reviewing Adithyan's development and design work. They are scanning for taste, technical range, clarity, and enough proof to start a conversation.

## Product Purpose

The portfolio presents Adithyan as a full-stack developer and designer who builds minimal, high-performance digital experiences. Success means visitors quickly understand the range of work, feel the craft of the interface, and know how to contact or evaluate him further.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, calm, precise. The experience should feel like a curated archive: restrained, typographic, intentional, and confident without becoming decorative or overproduced.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic card grids, bento layouts, filter-heavy galleries, loud SaaS visuals, oversized CTAs, stock-like decoration, and long project descriptions that make the portfolio feel like a template.

## Design Principles

- Let typography carry the first impression.
- Show range through structure, not clutter.
- Use motion as editorial pacing, not spectacle.
- Keep the system scalable as the project archive grows.
- Preserve accessibility and clarity across reduced motion, touch, and small screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast, keyboard-accessible project links, reduced-motion fallbacks, and touch behavior that exposes hover-only details on mobile.
